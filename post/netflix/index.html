Netflix Movie Duration Analysis

April 14, 2017

Project Overview:

The Netflix Movie Duration Analysis serves as a comprehensive exploration into the trends of movie durations on the Netflix platform. This data science capstone project commences with a background to the analysis, highlighting Netflix’s evolution from a DVD rental service to the largest entertainment and media company. The primary objective is to investigate the claim that the average duration of movies on Netflix has been declining.

Data Loading and Exploration:

The project begins by loading the necessary data, incorporating both aggregated data provided by a colleague (indicating average movie durations for the years 2011 to 2020) and a more extensive dataset obtained from the Netflix platform. A pandas DataFrame is utilized to structure and inspect the data.

Visual Inspection and Initial Analysis:

A line plot is employed to visualize the average movie durations over the specified years (2011-2020), providing an initial insight into the temporal trends. The project subsequently delves into a more detailed analysis by loading the entire Netflix dataset, enabling a more thorough exploration of movie durations and related attributes.

Data Filtering and Subset Creation:

To focus exclusively on movies, the dataset is filtered accordingly, and a subset of relevant columns (title, country, genre, release year, and duration) is created for subsequent analysis. This step ensures a more targeted examination of movie-specific attributes.

Visualizing Movie Durations:

The analysis progresses to visualizing movie durations using a scatter plot, showcasing the distribution of movie durations over an extended period. The scatter plot provides a clear depiction of trends and variations, allowing for a visual assessment of any temporal patterns.

Identifying Influential Genres:

An intriguing discovery emerges as shorter films, particularly genres such as “Children,” “Stand-Up,” and “Documentaries,” are identified as contributing significantly to the decline in average movie durations. These findings prompt a more in-depth investigation into the impact of non-typical genres on the observed trend.

Color-Coded Scatter Plot:

To enhance the visualization and highlight the influence of non-typical genres, a color-coded scatter plot is created. This innovative approach allows for the distinct marking of genres, providing a more granular understanding of how specific categories contribute to the overall trend.

Legend

  • Children : red
  • Documentaries : blue
  • Stand-Up : green
  • Others : black

Conclusion and Further Exploration:

The project concludes with a comprehensive summary of findings, emphasizing the significance of non-typical genres in shaping the observed decline in average movie durations on Netflix. The analysis sparks further curiosity, encouraging additional exploration and in-depth investigations to uncover the nuances of this trend.

Project Significance:

This Netflix Movie Duration Analysis serves as a robust example of an end-to-end data science capstone project. It encompasses data loading, cleaning, exploration, visualization, and interpretation, providing valuable insights for both data scientists and stakeholders interested in understanding the evolving landscape of movie durations on one of the world’s leading streaming platforms.
